Our Group's Contribution

Influencing Population Dynamics Through Local Action and School-Based Initiatives

Taking Action: From Learning to Doing

As students, we aim to apply our understanding of biotic potential and environmental resistance through simple, achievable school and community projects that make a real impact.

The Prevention Effort

Increasing Environmental Resistance
Managing pest populations by maintaining cleanliness and reducing breeding grounds.

Target Focus: Mosquitoes and Rats Around School

  • 1
    Clean-Up Drives
    Organize weekly clean-up drives in the school and nearby community to remove trash and potential pest habitats.
  • 2
    "No Standing Water" Check
    Inspect and cover containers that collect stagnant water to prevent mosquito breeding.
  • 3
    Proper Waste Disposal
    Encourage classmates and teachers to use closed trash bins and segregate waste properly.
  • 4
    Information Sharing
    Create posters and announcements on how simple habits can reduce pest problems around school.
100+ Students Participating
Visible Reduction in Pest Sightings
Impact:

By maintaining cleanliness and reducing standing water, we help increase environmental resistance, limiting the growth of pest populations in our school community.

The Conservation Effort

Decreasing Environmental Resistance
Supporting beneficial species by improving local green spaces and promoting biodiversity.

Target Focus: Local Birds and Pollinators

  • 1
    School Garden Project
    Plant native flowering plants and trees that provide food and shelter for pollinators and birds.
  • 2
    Recycling for Plant Pots
    Use recycled bottles and containers as planters to promote sustainability.
  • 3
    Awareness Campaign
    Conduct short classroom presentations or bulletin board displays on the importance of local wildlife.
  • 4
    Tree Planting Activity
    Coordinate with school administration to plant trees within or near the school grounds to provide habitats for birds.
50+ New Plants or Trees Grown
High Student Participation
Impact:

By creating green spaces and promoting awareness, we help decrease environmental resistance for beneficial species, allowing them to thrive within our school community.

Implementation Timeline

Week 1
Planning

Identify target areas in the school for cleaning and gardening. Assign group roles and prepare materials.

Week 2
Awareness Preparation

Design posters and infographics about pest prevention and conservation. Gather recyclable materials for planting.

Week 3
Action Week

Conduct the clean-up drive and "No Standing Water" check. Start planting in the school garden with recycled containers.

Week 4
Expansion & Education

Post awareness materials on bulletin boards. Conduct short presentations in classrooms about the importance of environmental care.

Week 5
Tree Planting

Coordinate with school administration for tree planting activity. Involve the wider school community in the conservation effort.

Week 6
Monitoring & Reflection

Observe and document changes in pest populations and beneficial species. Reflect on successes and areas for improvement.

Making a Real Difference

Through this two-pronged approach, our group will not only understand the scientific principles of population dynamics but actively influence them for positive environmental outcomes. By simultaneously increasing environmental resistance for pest species and decreasing it for endangered species, we demonstrate the practical application of ecological science in real-world conservation and management scenarios.
